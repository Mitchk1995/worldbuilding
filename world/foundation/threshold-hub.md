# Threshold Hub

Status: working design, not canon.

- This should be the last broadly recognizable route hub before the true frontier begins.
- It should sit where the central basin opens outward into harder sea and island traffic.
- It is the place where inland confidence, charter money, crews, sponsors, species mixing, and frontier rumor all collide.
- It should be busy enough to support ordinary life, trade, romance, and slower local futures.
- It should also carry the emotional weight of departures, recoveries, missing people, and delayed returns.
- This is where the center first starts to realize it does not actually understand the wider age.
- It should feel more competent and more mixed than the old center, but not yet like the deepest outer world.
- Tone: outwardly seductive, lively, and socially open, but underneath it runs on weathered competence and quiet grief.
- Social contrast: compared with the center, this place should feel more sexually open, less judgmental, and less trapped by sticky inherited ideologies.
- Surface importance: it is the main exchange point between inland wealth, coastal shipping, and outward crews.
- Deeper economy: recovery traffic, salvage, delayed returns, inherited goods, and the aftermath of failed expeditions are all part of how the place lives.
- Reinvention pull: people also come here to slip old identities, join crews, take lovers, disappear into a new life, or decide not to go back inland.
- It will likely be the best first concrete place to define before naming farther powers or regions.
