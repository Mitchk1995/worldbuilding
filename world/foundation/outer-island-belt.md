# Outer Island Belt

Status: working design, not canon.

## What the hub points toward first

- Beyond the threshold hub, the first true outward picture is not a continent but a broken belt of smaller islands, hard straits, and weather-shaped passages.
- It starts as a crowded outer maze still tied to hub traffic, then loosens into longer crossings where the gaps widen and every voyage feels less recoverable.
- Farther still, some islands become much larger again, but they are far enough apart that each one can reset what people think the outer world even is.
- The key feeling is layered widening. The world should keep opening instead of resolving into one clean map edge.

## Why this matters

- Newcomers leave expecting freedom and discovery. What they meet first is pilotage, tide judgment, storm memory, repair skill, and people who belong to hard water.
- This is the first place where central confidence fails in visible ways without the world becoming fully incomprehensible yet.
- The first outward shock should be other people's competence, not empty wilderness.
- The belt should feel inhabited, practical, and truth-tested before later discoveries become stranger.

## Scale discipline

- The old center should read as one large main island or island-heartland, not as an endless continent.
- Different directions can grow into different kinds of outer world later, but this belt is the default image attached to departure from the hub.
- Do not define a final outer limit.
- Do not answer yet whether the world is a planet, a layered construct, or something stranger.
- Leave room for later discoveries to widen the setting far beyond seas and islands, possibly even to system-scale or galaxy-scale horizons, without setting a ceiling now.
- Let later discoveries widen from hard archipelago travel to distant larger lands, and only much later to deeper cosmic questions.
